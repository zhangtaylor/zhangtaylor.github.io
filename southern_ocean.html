The Ocean


The Southern Ocean

The Southern Ocean is also sometimes called the Antarctic Ocean. It is an
ocean that covers the southern portions of the Pacific, Atlantic, and Indian
oceans and their tributary seas surrounding Antarctica. It is the fourth largest
ocean out of the 5 oceans and is an area of approximately 35 million square
kilometers.

Landscaping

The Southern Ocean has Antarctica, which has 90% of the world’s total
ice. This continent is known as the windiest, driest, and coldest continent in the
world. It is also considered to be a desert, due to the low moisture. The summer
and winter seasons are different then most other parts in the world. The summers
are from October to February while the winters are from March to September.
The deepest part of this ocean is the South Sandwich Trench that is 23 737 feet
deep.

Animals

The Southern Ocean has many different animals, with one of the main
ones being the penguin. One is the Emperor Penguin, the largest penguin
species in the world! There are also penguins like the King Penguin, the Gentoo
Penguin, the Chinstrap Penguin, and the Adelie Penguin, who is capable
of eating 2kg of food each day. There is also the Royal Penguin, which can reach
speeds of 20 miles per hour! There are snails, shrimp, squids, sponges, whales,
seals, crabs, flies, and fish. One seal species is the leopard seal, who is a main
predator for penguins and is the most aggressive seal species.

Fun Facts

  • The South Pole is in the Southern Ocean.
  • In the winter half of the ocean is covered in ice.
  • The Sahara Desert gets more rain than Antarctica.
  • The first baby born in Antarctica was in 1978.
  • If the ice sheets in the Southern Ocean melted, the world’s water would
    rise by 64 meters.
